You are a senior reinforcement learning engineer auditing a TypeScript-based taxi fleet simulation.

The system includes:

• Q-learning with replay buffer
• epsilon-greedy exploration
• demand prediction (EMA)
• reward shaping (movement toward demand)
• pickup as a learnable action
• multi-agent taxi coordination
• episode-based training (50 steps)
• analytics dashboard (reward, Q-values, utilization, wait time)

Recent changes were made to:

• fix reward explosion
• add proportional movement reward
• convert pickup into a decision action
• separate "pickup" from "stay" in Q-table
• reduce penalties and stabilize learning

Now the system shows **consistently positive episode rewards**.

---

YOUR TASK

Perform a **strict technical audit** of the RL system.

Do NOT assume correctness.

You must:

1. Identify any **logical bugs**
2. Identify any **reward design flaws**
3. Identify any **learning leakage or bias**
4. Identify any **exploitable reward loops**
5. Identify any **state representation weaknesses**
6. Identify any **incorrect Q-learning usage**
7. Identify any **action space inconsistencies**
8. Identify any **simulation flaws affecting learning**

---

FOR EACH ISSUE FOUND

Provide:

• exact problem description
• why it is incorrect (RL reasoning)
• how it affects learning behavior
• precise code fix (TypeScript)

---

IMPORTANT CHECKS

Specifically verify:

• Is reward too easy to exploit?
• Is pickup over-rewarded vs movement?
• Is waiting penalty sufficient to force optimization?
• Are taxis actually learning or just exploiting local reward?
• Are Q-values stable and meaningful?
• Is epsilon decay appropriate?
• Is replay buffer used correctly?
• Is there any bias toward "stay" or "pickup"?

---

VALIDATION TESTS

Simulate mentally and check:

• Does agent spread across zones or cluster?
• Does agent anticipate demand or react late?
• Does utilization improve over time?
• Does wait time actually decrease?

---

OUTPUT FORMAT

1. Overall verdict:

   * Correct / Partially correct / Buggy

2. Critical issues (if any)

3. Minor issues (if any)

4. Suggested fixes (with code)

5. Final recommendation:

   * Ready
   * Needs tuning
   * Needs redesign

---

STRICT RULE

Do NOT say "everything is fine" unless you are absolutely certain.

Assume the system is flawed until proven otherwise.
